LinkedIn’s decision to incorporate games into the platform may seem like chance, but in fact Daniel Roth, their editor in chief and VP claims it was not accidental. According to Roth,
Our goal at LinkedIn is to continue to find ways to bring professionals together to stay informed and connected...We want to give people a way to exercise their brains while taking a quick break, but also give people a reason to connect with others. We hope that these games spark banter, conversations, and even a healthy bit of competition among professionals around the world.
At the moment, you can participate in three games currently:
  • Pinpoint: Let us look at an example of a word association game where players get a set of five words. The objective is to correctly identify which category these words fall under with the least number of guesses.
  • Queens: Sudoku with queens is a variation of Sudoku that uses chess pieces rather than numbers. To solve this problem involves putting queens on the chessboard so that no two queens can attack each other and having one queen in each row.
  • Crossclimb: Crossclimb combines quizzes about facts and jokes with words, starting from one word then converting it into another one by changing just one letter after that.
